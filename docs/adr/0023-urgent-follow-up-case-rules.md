# Urgent Follow-up Case marking for SMS and external service

Hermes marks a **Follow-up Case** as urgent when SMS or other external customer-service interactions involve:

- billing disputes or claims of payment not reflected in records
- clear emotional escalation or complaint threats
- safety-sensitive issues involving people, warehouse, or delivery safety
- repeated tool failures while the customer keeps pressing for an account-specific answer

Hermes does not mark routine order-status questions, general policy or hours questions, standard Payment Link requests, or standard unmatched-caller cases as urgent by default.

Urgent cases appear at the top of the **Copilot Workbench** queue. Hermes may tell the customer the request was flagged for priority review but must not promise a specific response time.

**Considered options:** no urgent flag in SMS because it is 24/7 (rejected—human follow-up still needs prioritization); urgent for every unmatched caller (rejected—too noisy).
